Places to See, Things to Do

Just a few examples of the many things to do in or around DC and Baltimore

Baltimore area

Love animals?

Check out the National Aquarium.
Protip: Don't sit in the splash zone at the dolphin show if you're not willing to get wet.

Love history?

Visit the site of the inspiration for our National Anthem, Ft. McHenry.

Love baseball?

Visit one of the most beautiful stadiums in the game, Camden Yards. Don't miss the Babe Ruth Birthplace Museum and the Sports Legends Museum at Camden Yards.

Washington, DC area

Love museums?

There's a Smithsonian museum for everyone, whether you're interested in animals, space, art, history, or other topics. Best of all, all the Smithsonian museums in DC are free.

Love the outdoors?

Take a walk around the National Mall area. Many memorials (including the Lincoln Memorial, the World War II Memorial, and the Vietnam Veterans Memorial) are within walking distance. If the weather's nice there are also many companies that run walking, biking, or Segway tours that guide you around the area.

Love theater?

Take in a performance at the Kennedy Center. There are also a number of other excellent theaters in the area, including: the National Theatre, the Folger Theatre, Ford's Theatre, and Arena Stage that will likely have shows during your time here.

Our Story

Here's how our journey started.

  • It started with a song

    Ty and I met one day when a certain first soprano spotted a scruffy looking tenor who was new to choir. Even with his glasses she noticed his eyes...a beautiful blue.

  • Friendship

    We got to know each other better as part of an awesome group of friends here in the Washington, DC area. Camping, tubing, laser tag--all the shenanigans.

  • Our relationship deepens

    Out picking pumpkins from a pumpkin patch - there was a spark. When we started dating, no one was really surprised. True, most of our choir friends sighed and said "FINALLY!"

  • Engaged!

    So, I'll admit I had my suspicions about a random trip to New Orleans (like you do), but when Ty took me to dinner at Cochon and a champagne glass with a ring found its way to me - I could not have been more surprised or pleased! I'm pretty sure you could have heard a pin drop in that restaurant when he asked me marry him and everyone waited for the response. Of course I said "yes"! Easy question.
